# English Premier League 2025/26 Season Analytics Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

This project is a comprehensive 6-page interactive Power BI dashboard analysing the complete 2025/26 Premier League season.

Built using real match data covering all 380 league fixtures, the dashboard transforms raw football statistics into actionable insights covering team performance, title progression, attacking and defensive efficiency, discipline trends, and betting market intelligence.

The project demonstrates advanced Power BI skills including data cleaning, transformation, modelling, visualisation, and business storytelling.

---

## 🎯 Business Context

The Premier League is the world's most watched football league, generating billions of pounds annually through broadcasting rights, sponsorships, merchandise sales, and betting markets.

Data analytics plays a crucial role for:

- Football clubs and coaching staff
- Sports media organisations
- Betting operators
- Fantasy football platforms
- Performance analysts

This dashboard demonstrates how football match data can be transformed into meaningful insights that support strategic decision-making.

## 📂 Data Source

Data was sourced from Football-Data.co.uk — a trusted repository of historical football match data used by analysts, researchers and betting professionals worldwide.

The raw dataset contained 132 columns which were cleaned, filtered and reduced to 39 meaningful columns for this analysis.

# 📄 Dashboard Pages

## 🏆 Page 1 — Season Overview & Final Standings

![Season Overview](EPL%20page%201%20season%20overview.png)

### Purpose

Provides a complete overview of the season's outcomes and league standings.

### Key Insights

- Arsenal won the Premier League title with 85 points.
- Arsenal finished 7 points ahead of Manchester City.
- The season produced 930 goals.
- Home teams won 162 matches.
- Sunderland achieved an impressive 7th-place finish after promotion.
- West Ham, Burnley and Wolves were relegated.

## 📈 Page 2 — Title Race & Form Timeline

![Title Race & Form](EPL%20page%202%20Title%20race%20and%20form.png)

### Purpose

Tracks team momentum, title progression and seasonal turning points.

### Key Insights

- Arsenal led the league from Gameweek 8 onwards.
- Manchester City's decline in March proved decisive.
- Arsenal's strongest month was December 2025.
- Bournemouth secured European qualification after finishing 6th.
- Tottenham recorded one of their lowest league finishes in decades.

## ⚽ Page 3 — Attack & Defence Analysis

![Attack & Defence Analysis](EPL%20page%203%20attack%20and%20defense.png)

### Purpose

Compares attacking efficiency and defensive strength across all Premier League teams.

### Key Insights

- Manchester City scored the most goals.
- Arsenal recorded the fewest goals conceded.
- Arsenal achieved the league's best goal difference.
- Brentford posted the highest shot accuracy.
- Chelsea and Manchester City featured in the highest percentage of Over 2.5 goal matches.

## 🟨 Page 5 — Discipline & Referees

![Discipline & Referees](EPL%20Page%205%20Discipline%20and%20referee.png)

### Purpose

Analyses disciplinary records and referee behaviour throughout the season.

### Key Insights

- December recorded the highest disciplinary activity.

## 💰 Page 6 — Betting & Odds Intelligence

![Betting & Odds Intelligence](EPL%20Page%206%20Betting%20and%20odds.png)

### Purpose

Examines bookmaker expectations, betting trends and market efficiency.

### Key Insights

- Favourites won only 48% of matches.
- Chelsea featured most frequently in Over 2.5 goal matches.
- Burnley and Wolves consistently entered matches as underdogs.
- Pinnacle and Bet365 odds showed strong market alignment.
- Arsenal's defeat to Bournemouth represented one of the season's biggest upsets.

## 🧹 Data Cleaning & Preparation

The dataset underwent extensive cleaning and transformation in Power Query:

- Converted date fields into proper date format using UK locale settings.
- Renamed abbreviated column headers to descriptive field names.
- Applied correct data types across all columns.
- Created Month-Year fields for chronological analysis.
- Performed data quality validation checks.
- Preserved null values where necessary to maintain data integrity.
- Reduced the dataset from 132 columns to 39 analysis-ready columns.
